many years ago Quietype over on WithTheWill came up with the original card templates, and also eyeballed the fonts in use on the official cards. he probably didn't expect that lots of people would stick to his initial thoughts years later.

this is a guide on reverse-engineering the fonts in question.

if you want to suggest a new font, specifically cite at least 1 glyph that is a better match to the card text. if that causes some other glyphs to be wrong, that's okay, we won't have to use the new one but knowing what pieces we need is valuable

if a font on this page isn't loading, it will default to Times New Roman like this

Ayar Kasone looks exactly right. Helvetica is a decent backup that looks nice on the "7" but fails on the "0".

Effect Text

current confidence: medium-high outside keyword, high inside keyword

There are two different fonts in use on effect text. One is the raw text, the other is that inside of effects.

The effect text outside has straight lower-case L and the lower-case G has a hook. Inside of a colored-block, the l is curved and the g is hooked.

Roboto Condensed is a decent match here. The comma too curved and the "t" and "h" have sloped tops.
Asimov Print C seems a bit better. The "t" and "h" are missing the slopes on top.
For the text inside the effect box, Falling Sky seems a perfect match.

Traits

current confidence: low

The trait text is harder to see. The capital-U is a perfect curiive, the lower-case U has a prong hanging off the lower-right and the lower-case G has a prong on the upper-right. The vertical strokes on the capital-M are not straight up-and-down.

Repo Medium sort of matches, except that the lower-case U and lower-case G are missing prongs.
